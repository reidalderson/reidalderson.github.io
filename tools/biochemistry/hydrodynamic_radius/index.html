Hydrodynamic radius

Hydrodynamic radius

Hydrodynamic radius


NMR pulsed-field-gradient translational diffusion experiments enable measurement of the hydrodynamic radius (Rh) of a molecule of interest. This is typically performed by comparing the translational diffusion coefficient of a given molecule to a standard of a known Rh, e.g. dioxane. It is important that (1) the standard does not interact with the molecule of interest and (2) the NMR signals from the standard are clearly resolved. If these criteria are met, then the ratio of the diffusion coefficients between sample and the standard (sample/standard) multiplied by the Rh of the standard yields the Rh of the sample.

standard_Rh = 2.12 # Rh of the standard (Angstroms)
standard_D = 8e-6 # Diffusion coefficient of the standard (cm^2 s-1)
sample_D = 1e-6	# Diffusion coefficient of the sample (cm^2 s-1)
sample_Rh = standard_Rh * (standard_D / sample_D) # Rh of the sample, (Angstroms)
print('>>>',sample_Rh,'Angstroms')

>>> 16.96 Angstroms

By using the above procedure, Wilkins et al. (1999 Biochemistry, 38: 16424-31) measured the Rh values for a series of proteins that were either folded or chemically denatured. The authors derived empirical relations between the number of amino acids in a protein and the Rh, for both folded and unfolded states of a protein.
